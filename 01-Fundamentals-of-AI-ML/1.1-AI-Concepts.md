## 3. Exam Focus & Key Concepts

### A. Learning Methods
These methods describe how the ML model learns from data:

| Method | Data Type | Goal | AWS Example Use Case |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Supervised Learning** | **Labeled Data** (Input paired with desired Output) | **Prediction** (e.g., predicting a continuous number) or **Classification** (e.g., predicting a category). | Predicting a house price (Regression) or classifying an email as Spam/Not Spam (Classification). |
| **Unsupervised Learning** | **Unlabeled Data** (Input only) | **Clustering** or discovering hidden patterns and structures in the data. | Segmenting customers into groups for marketing (Clustering). |
| **Reinforcement Learning (RL)** | **No specific data**; learning through trial-and-error via **Rewards** and **Penalties** (feedback). | Optimizing a control system or making a sequence of decisions to maximize a long-term goal. | Optimizing the route for a delivery fleet (AWS DeepRacer). |

### B. The ML Process & Data Types
* **Training:** The iterative process where the model adjusts its internal parameters (weights and biases) to minimize the difference between its prediction and the actual, known outcome.
* **Inferencing (Prediction):** The process of using a **trained** model to make a prediction or decision on new, **unseen** data.
    * **Real-time Inference:** Used for immediate, low-latency predictions (e.g., fraud detection at the moment of a credit card swipe).
    * **Batch Inference:** Used for making predictions on large volumes of data at scheduled intervals (e.g., generating end-of-day sales forecasts).
* **Data Types:**
    * **Labeled Data:** Data where the answer (target) is known and attached to the input (required for Supervised Learning).
    * **Unlabeled Data:** Data without an attached answer (used for Unsupervised Learning and Foundational Model pre-training).
    * **Structured Data:** Organized data, typically in tables (rows and columns).
    * **Unstructured Data:** Data without a defined format (e.g., images, video, free-form text).
